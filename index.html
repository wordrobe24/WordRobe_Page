Method

We propose WordRobe, a novel method for the controllable generation of 3D textured garments from natural language text prompts. Our method can generate high-quality unposed 3D garments with photorealistic textures, in an optimization-free inference step. We achieve this by first learning a latent space of 3D garments using a novel two-stage encoder-decoder framework following a coarse-to-fine training strategy, representing the 3D garments as unsigned distance fields (UDF). We introduce an additional loss function to further disentangle the garment's latent space, enabling better control over latent manipulation for garment editing. The learned latent space is then aligned with the CLIP space by learning a mapping network to allow text-driven generation. We devise a novel weakly-supervised training scheme that eliminates the need for explicit manual text annotations for training the mapping network. Subsequently, we propose a novel, optimization-free text-driven synthesis of texture to allow view-consistent generation, thereby drastically decreasing the generation time as compared to existing optimization-based methods. We also curate a dataset of 50k high-quality 3D garments in different textures and corresponding text prompts.
